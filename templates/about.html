What is Alexandria?

Alexandria is a constrained, agent-guided reading system.

It is not:

  • ChatGPT with citations
  • A PDF reader with AI
  • A knowledge graph
  • A recommendation engine
  • A social reading app
  • A place for "answers"

How it works

You bring confusion or questions. The system routes you to primary source texts — books, chapters, passages. LLMs act only as guides, never as authorities.

If you can walk away without reading, the system has failed.

The rules

  1. Canon is sacred. Books and text are immutable. Users cannot edit texts. LLMs cannot modify canon.
  2. Reading is primary. No default summaries. No global chat. No answer-first UI. Everything resolves to text.
  3. LLMs are tools, not minds. Stateless. Deterministic. Replaceable. Constrained by contracts.

The constraint is the product

Modern LLMs hallucinate and abstract. People want to read the source, not interpretations. Intellectual honesty requires constraint.

This is intentional.
